Breast Cancer Awareness

Supportive information every woman can use

Early awareness matters. Knowing the common symptoms, healthy lifestyle habits, and when to seek screening can support faster action and better outcomes.

Awareness Poster

Visual guide inspired by women's health campaigns

Symptoms

Common warning signs

Lump or thickened tissue

A new lump in the breast or underarm is one of the most common signs that should be checked by a doctor.

Change in breast size or shape

Noticeable swelling, unusual asymmetry, or a sudden change in contour can be a reason to seek medical advice.

Skin changes

Look for dimpling, redness, scaling, irritation, or skin that starts to resemble an orange peel texture.

Nipple changes or discharge

Inversion, pain, or unexpected discharge, especially if bloody, should be evaluated promptly.

Prevention

Healthy habits that may lower risk

1

Maintain a healthy weight

Regular movement and balanced nutrition can support hormone balance and overall breast health.

2

Exercise regularly

Aim for consistent physical activity each week, even if it starts with walking, yoga, or home workouts.

3

Limit alcohol and avoid smoking

Reducing these risk factors can help lower the chance of several cancers, including breast cancer.

4

Follow screening advice

Talk with a clinician about family history, mammograms, and the right screening plan for your age and risk level.

Self Care

Simple breast-health reminders

Know your normal

Become familiar with how your breasts usually look and feel so changes are easier to notice.

Do regular self-checks

Self-checks do not replace screening, but they can help you notice changes early and seek guidance faster.

Seek help early

Any unusual symptom deserves attention. Early consultation is often more valuable than waiting to see if it goes away.
